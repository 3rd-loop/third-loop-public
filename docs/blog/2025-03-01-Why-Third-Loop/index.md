Third Loop is not built for the masses. It's built for technical knowledge workers who demand high performance and know exactly what they need from their tools.

<!-- truncate -->

## What Our Users Look Like

We started Third Loop with a mission to create AI solutions for engineers and consultants who solve complex, technical problems. Our users aren't typically from software backgrounds. They do extensive technical writing, work in specialized analysis tools, and think big picture: strategy, risk, business analysis.

They face demanding cybersecurity requirements driven by the nature of their work and their commitments to clients. They need to share data within their organizations, but often only within specific project teams. They're intelligent, know what they want, and know where to find information.

Our target users are high-performance teams that need AI and software tools that keep them in control of their process. They need to codify workflows. They need organizational consistency. They need to cite sources and track information provenance. They need private data repositories that don't cross-pollinate between projects. And they need custom workflows they want to build but lack the resources to create.

## Distilled Customer Requirements

If I had to distill the requirements down to three bullet points:

- A chat interface with deep functionality, not autonomous functionality. Tools that give users significant control over the process.
- Customized, repeatable workflows. Purpose-built for their projects with consistency baked in.
- Top-tier security. Just to get past their IT department.

## The Alternatives

Most office workers have used ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, or similar tools. These platforms offer enterprise support and data security agreements, but they're horizontal products designed for everyone, which means they're optimized for no one in particular.

They offer no engineering or consulting workflows out of the box. ChatGPT is focused on adding ads and Walmart plugins, not engineering workflows. They don't know your industry's documentation, codes, or delivery standards. All three enterprise tiers guarantee your data is never used for model training, so privacy isn't a differentiator. But that doesn't mean they meet the specific requirements for your cyber security or quality assurance program. Plus, if you want industry-specific capability, you'll need engineers to build it yourself, which brings us to the other alternative.

Building in-house sounds like control. In practice, it's a second business.

A team of 3–5 engineers costs $500K–$1.5M annually in salary alone, before compute, infrastructure, security reviews, compliance work, or maintenance. More importantly, it never ends. Every model update, every new integration, every compliance requirement becomes your team's problem indefinitely.

Engineering and consulting firms don't exist to build and maintain AI infrastructure. Your competitive advantage is your people's expertise and your client relationships, not your ability to ship internal software.

## The Third Path

Our platform is the third option: the workflows, integrations, and industry knowledge you'd spend years building, ready on day one.

Third Loop isn't just licensed software. It's a fully supported platform with training, deployment flexibility, and collaboration tools built in.

**Data & Collections.** We already have many of the data resources you need built in. Industry documentation, codes, and standards relevant to your practice are pre-loaded so you're not starting from scratch. Your team adds their own curated knowledge base, and your past work makes the system smarter over time. Need more resources? We can help make that happen.

**Workflows.** Structured processes for your most common deliverables, built around how engineering and consulting work actually gets done. Reduce time from brief to first draft, and ensure outputs are consistent across your team.

**Collaboration & Reviews.** Built-in approval chains and document sharing for formal or informal reviews. Every output includes structured documentation and audit trails, so work is traceable, reviewable, and defensible. This matters when liability is on the line.

**Model Flexibility.** Choose from cutting-edge models (GPT-4, Claude, and more) per workflow or agent. You're not locked into one vendor's approach. Your team picks the right tool for each task.

**Agent Builder.** Your technical staff can create custom tools for specific workflows without building infrastructure from scratch. The power of a development platform, without the overhead of maintaining one.

**Secure Sandbox.** Engineers can prototype and test in an isolated environment before anything touches production or client data. Build fast, deploy responsibly.

**SDK.** Embed our capabilities directly into the tools your team already uses. Build custom integrations to your existing databases and systems without needing our engineering team. We fit your workflow; you don't have to change yours to fit ours.

**Deployment Options.** Available on commercial cloud or GovCloud to meet your compliance requirements. Whether you're working on federal contracts or commercial projects, we have the infrastructure you need.

**Training & Adoption.** Industry-specific onboarding, video libraries, and live webinars included with every contract. We help your team actually adopt the platform, not just license it. When you sign up, you get training tailored to engineers and consultants, not generic AI tutorials.

**Domain Expertise and Support.** We work for engineers and consultants. We are engineers and consultants. When you have a question, you talk to someone who understands your business, not a generic support queue.
